Make Your Health A Habit
Alright, this is a big one. So listen up! Making a habit change is hard. Like really hard. But it’s 100 times easier when you understand how habits work.
“A habit is something we do with little or no conscious thought.“
Habits are great because we reduce the mental power it requires to complete a task. Think about something you do every day, like brushing your teeth, walking the dog, or packing your lunch for work. to ponder the pros and cons of what you do carefully.
You just do it. With little or no conscious thought.
This mental shortcut is the essence of a habit. And we’re going to help you start building healthy habits and ditching ones that are holding you back for your goal.

4 Tips To Create A New Habit
1. Be consistent. Consistency is key to any habit or lifestyle change. Make the habit every day and as close to the same time as possible.
2. Stick to it for 30 days. Habits are harder to build in the beginning because we’re fighting against existing habits. Sticking strong for the first 30 days will be the hardest, but
3. Hook into a current habit. Use an existing habit as an anchor for a new habit your trying to create. The formula goes something like this, “When I ___, then I will ____.”
“When I get home from work, then I will go to the gym for a workout.”
“When I get ready for bed, then I will listen to an episode of Pursoo.”
“When I get a break at work, then I will walk with my coworkers.”
4. Create a daily reminder. You’re going to get busy some days. Luckily technology makes it easier than ever to keep focused when life throws everything it can at you. Create a reminder for yourself, any reminder!
